Module 5: Reclaiming Treaty Narratives

Summary

Objectives

  • Explain how the media portrays the relationship between land and identity within Indigenous societies.
  • Develop a complex understanding of treaties from multiple historical and personal narratives
  • Explore how Indigenous Peoples fought for their rights in historic and modern contexts

Summary

This module should have provided a historical framework of how the Treaty process shaped Indigenous realities. You should have an understanding of why and how Indigenous People are taking back their narratives. You should now have a historical framework of the Treaty process shaped Indigenous realities and understand why Indigenous People are taking back their narratives

Test your Knowledge

Why do many Indigenous Peoples view the treaties as a trick?

The British settlers verbally told the Indigenous Peoples one thing but the written document Indigenous Leaders signed contained different information than what was verbally agreed upon.

What are Oral Histories?

Indigenous histories, stories, and cultures are told in their own words passed down from generation to generation.
