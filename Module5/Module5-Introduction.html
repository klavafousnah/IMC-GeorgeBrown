Introduction to Module 5

This module will investigate how assimilation manifests racism against Indigenous Peoples and Nations. The development of colonial policies under the Indian Act creates narratives in which Indigenous People are unable to manage their own affairs and are forcibly removed from their traditional territories.

The primary goal of treaties was to deny Indigenous Peoples their right to land. For government interests, the media would frequently favour misrepresenting Indigenous People. Indigenous People stereotypes aid in the construction of a plausible narrative for Canadian settlers. Indigenous peoples are discredited as intelligent, complex, and the original inhabitants of North America as a result of these stereotypes. The media portrays Indigenous People in a variety of stereotypes, primarily because they are unable to control and are responsible for their own lives.
