Module 5: Nation-to-Nation Narratives

Learning Objectives

By the end of the module you will be able to:

  • Describe the significance of Indigenous storytelling.
  • Relate examples of the oral tradition of Indigenous Peoples in relation to the land.
  • Explain how Indigenous perspectives are shaped by the land.
  • Explain the impact of colonial narratives that perpetuate misinformation, racism, and stereotypes on both Indigenous communities and the broader society.
  • Examine government policies and practices that are inherently racist.
  • Examine current and historical examples of racism in relation to Indigenous Peoples.

End of the week check: I am able understand the importance of Nation-to-Nation Narratives

Introduction to Module 5

This module will look at more current examples of racism as well as popular (mis)representations of Indigenous Peoples. In the early 1990s, the Oka Crisis altered the political landscape and solidified how the Canadian government interacts with Indigenous Peoples. More blockades occurred, and reports have been released detailing how the Canadian government (mis)treats Indigenous Peoples. Here you have an opportunity to delve deeper into the various representations and perceptions of treaties and how the land and treaties shape Indigenous and non-Indigenous relationships.
