Introduction to Kanehsatake: 270 Years of Resistance:

The documentary film Kanehsatake: 270 Years of Resistance, by Alanis Obomsawin, was produced by the National Film Board of Canada, after first being rejected by the CBC. This film is critical to understanding contemporary land claims in Canada and important in terms of bringing Indigenous narratives into the mainstream. It offers a lot to unpack as it explores relationships between the federal, provincial, and municipal layers of government, the military (the army, the Sûreté du Quebec), Indian Affairs, government representatives (Tom Siddon (federal), John Ciaccia (provincial), and Jean Ouellette (the mayor of Oka)), the townspeople of Oka, as well as the tribal council, warrior society (protectors of women and children), and residents of Kanehsatake.
