Course Overview

Indigenous People in the Media

Course Outcomes and Learning Objectives

Outcome #1
Analyze the role of mainstream media in perpetuating the colonial narrative that adversely impacts Indigenous communities.

Objectives
  • Analyze the impact of media bias and manipulation of information on Indigenous people and their stories.
  • Explain how popular media, especially the news media uses storytelling to perpetuate the continued oppression of Indigenous Peoples.
  • Explain how storytelling was used in Indigenous Oral Traditions.
  • Contrast perceptions of colonialism
  • Relate colonial policies to contemporary Indigenous contexts.
  • Analyze examples of assimilationist policies in relation to Indigenous families.
  • Analyze contemporary assertions of Indigenous sovereignty, self-determination and sustainability.
    • Outcome #2
    Explain how the media portrays the relationship between land and identity within Indigenous societies.

    Objectives
  • Describe the significance of Indigenous storytelling.
  • Apply concepts of responsibility to community development
  • Create a code of ethics based on the Anishnaabe Seven Grandfather Teachings
  • Relate examples of oral tradition of Indigenous people in relation to the land.
  • Investigate the significance of traditional ecological knowledge.
    • Outcome #3
    Explain how the media’s colonial ideas about diversity perpetuate myths of Indigenous diversity.

    Objectives
  • Describe the inclusion and diversity from an Indigenous perspective.
  • Describe Canadian assumptions of Indigenous diversity.
  • Explain how Indigenous perspectives are shaped by the land.
  • Describe how colonial myths of diversity affect….
    • Outcome #4
    Analyze racism inherent in the media in relation to Indigenous peoples.

    Objectives
  • Explain how the impact of the colonial narrative which perpetuates misinformation, racism, and stereotypes affects both Indigenous communities and the broader society.
    • Examine government policies and practices that are inherently racist.
  • Examine current and historical examples of racism in relation to Indigenous peoples.
  • Examine common misrepresentations of Indigenous people.
  • Analyze the concept of privilege.
    • Outcome #5
    a) Describe ways to implement TRC media-focused Calls-to-Action.
    b) Propose ways to centre the truth in reconciliation practices.
