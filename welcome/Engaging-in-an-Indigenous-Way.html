Engaging in an Indigenous Way

Seven Grandfather Teachings

If you are unfamiliar with the Seven Grandfather Teachings, explore these prior to starting the course. They will give you some additional context to think about as you explore the course. 

The Seven Grandfather Teachings – Historical and Contemporary Realities: Movement Towards Reconciliation (pressbooks.pub)  

Apply this to your course experience

This course is built in opposition to a western way of thinking about knowledge. You will not need to show your instructor or peers all of the work that you complete in this course, but please share within the discussion forums. This course focuses on self-reflection and building trust between you and your instructor. Use the Seven Grand Father Teachings to inform the way you engage with content and complete activities within this course.

The Seven Grandfather Teachings of the Anishnaabe peoples are Wisdom, Love, Respect, Bravery, Honesty, Humility, and Truth. This course incorporates some of these ideas in the way you are reflecting on the knowledge in this course.

Wisdom: You should be able to speak well and to take well (what) someone says. When responding to a post do so from the lens of respect.

Love: It is your turn, you should love yourself before you love somebody who is different. Incorporate self-love and care into everything you create.

Respect: You should think well of your teacher and peers. Create your assignments and posts with respect for your peers.

Bravery: Be brave. Hold firm in your thoughts and stand strong even when you don’t know what will happen right now. Be willing to confront stereotypes you may already have.

Honesty: Be honest first with yourself, and you will more easily be able to be honest with others. When creating posts, make sure that you speak from your heart in addition to your mind.

Humility: Remember that you are equal to others, but you are not better. Reflect on this while creating your assignments and responding to discussion posts.

Truth: Truth is to know all of these things. Speak the truth. Do not deceive yourself or others. Express these throughout your interaction with this course.

You are allowed and encouraged to step out of the westernized educational environment you commonly exist in for other courses. Push yourself to engage with the content in personally meaningful ways that go beyond learning the content of the course.
