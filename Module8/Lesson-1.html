Module 8: Sharing our stories

Lesson 1: Develop a historical context of various Indigenous medias

Objectives

  • Explore the impact of Indigenous stereotypes
  • Understand the connection between historical events (ex. the Numbered Treaties, Resedential Schools) and Indigenous representation in the media
  • Understand why Indigenous narratives in media are important

Journal Questions

How does police presence differ at BIPOC led protests and marches to police presence at white led protests? Explore how the Canadian police system is systematically racist.

How does ‘nîpawistamâsowin: We Will Stand Up’, an Indigenous narrated documentary, compare to non-Indigenous led narratives about Indigenous Peoples you have encountered previously? Why are Indigenous media sources so important?

Explore how Indigenous stereotypes perpetuated by the media can wrongly paint Indigenous Peoples as criminal. How did these stereotypes impact the trial of Gerald Stanley? How did they impact Colten Boushie’s family?

Uni: How does the Canadian Justice System fail Indigenous people? Explore this in modern and historic contexts.
