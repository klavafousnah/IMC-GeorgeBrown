Introduction to Module 8

This module summarizes the reasons why it is critical for Indigenous Peoples to tell their stories about current events that have occurred in their communities, and how they can do so effectively. Students will investigate why the narrative about settlers' values of the land is more important than the lives of Indigenous People. One of the objectives is to demonstrate that these narratives have been fabricated by settlers in order to continue taking resources from Indigenous People's traditional territories.

Indigenous Peoples are reclaiming control of the narratives and retracing their steps back to the beginning to explain why there are problems in the Nation-to-Nation relationships today. Students will gain an understanding of the significance of Indigenous people using various media platforms for activism, as well as the reasons for their participation. Students will be guided through the process of understanding how systemic racism and structural oppression further marginalize Indigenous Peoples.

Why are Indigenous new sources, like APTN, significant?

Indigenous Peoples having control over telling their own narratives takes power back from settlers who have (mis)told Indigenous stories for years. It also allows for the important intergreation of Indigenous cultures and languages into media.
