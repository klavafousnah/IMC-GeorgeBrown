Skywoman Falling

Introduction to “Skywoman Falling”:

“Skywoman Falling” is a short rendition of one of the stories shared by the Haudenosaunee Nation. It shares the Creation view of Haudenosaunee People and the Original Instructions bestowed upon the Indigenous Peoples of Turtle Island. It also re-enforces Indigenous pedagogies around sitting and listening to stories being shared, setting the tone for listening to stories in this course.

Questions to Consider

1. In the story, ‘Skywoman Falling’--the indigenous Creation story (pp. 3-10), you learn that Skywoman lived as if her children’s future mattered. If you truly focus on the Earth that will be left for your grandchildren, how would you live differently?

2. In Native ways of knowing, human people are often referred to as “the younger brothers of Creation.” (p. 9) Do you agree that humans can learn from plants and animals?

3. How can we humble ourselves to ‘listen’ to the wisdom of the plants?
