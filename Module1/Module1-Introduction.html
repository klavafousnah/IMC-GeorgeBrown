Module 1: Creation Story

Understand the importance of Indigenous Creation stories.

Objectives

  • Describe the significance of Indigenous storytelling.
  • Apply concepts of responsibility to community development
  • ‘Apply’ the code of ethics based on the Anishnaabe Seven Grandfather Teachings
  • Relate examples of oral tradition of Indigenous people in relation to the land.
  • Investigate the significance of traditional ecological knowledge.

Introducution to Module 1

Many Indigenous cultures pass down Creation stories to future generations because they are critical to understanding who they are and where they came from. This module begins with Indigenous Creation stories because they focus on how the world was formed, how people were situated, and relationships with the land. It frames the historical context of Indigenous perspectives and a reverence for the land as a living, breathing entity.

There are common threads in Indigenous Creation stories, but it is important to note that each story is unique to its culture’s Oral tradition dictated that Creation stories be told in their entirety over the course of several days, allowing listeners ample time to process the vastness of the cosmos. The goal is to provide students with a solid understanding of the relationship between land and identity within Indigenous Nations across Turtle Island. The goal is to give students a solid understanding of the relationship between land and culture among the various Indigenous peoples living on Turtle Island.

Within these modules, it will privilege Indigenous narratives. This will re-tell some of the historical accounts but through an Indigenous lens that has not always been shared before in mainstream media. The course will share stories about Indigenous Peoples from their perspectives or will critique popular representations and historical accounts.
