Module Introduction

All Indigenous Peoples pass down Creation stories to future generations. They are critical to understanding who we are and where we came from. This module begins with Indigenous Creation stories because they focus on how the world was formed, how people were situated, and relationships with the land. They also frame the historical context for Indigenous perspectives and a reverence for the land as a living, breathing entity.

There are common threads in Indigenous Creation stories, but it is important to note that each story is unique to its Nation and its Nation’s oral traditions. Creation stories are told in their entirety over the course of several days, allowing listeners ample time to process the complexity of the cosmos. The goal of the stories is to provide a foundation for understanding the relationship between land and identity within Indigenous Nations across Turtle Island.
