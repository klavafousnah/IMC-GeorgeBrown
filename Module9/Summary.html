Module 9: Rise of Indigenous Reporting

Summary

Objectives

  • Understand the importance of Indigenous medias by and for Indigenous Peoples
  • Explore how Indigenous narratives can impact settler understandings of Nation-to-Nation relationships
  • Identify how Indigenous Peoples are using media to take back their narratives

Summary

Indigenous reporting and journalism have proven to be an important and powerful tool in changing and taking back narratives relating to Indigenous Peoples. You should now understand how Indigenous journalism can change the bias against Indigenous Peoples that has always existed in journalism and media. Indigenous journalism has the power to amplify Indigenous voices and narratives while also providing settlers an opportunity to challenge racist and colonial narratives that have been prioritized in media.

Test your Knowledge

What does Connie Walker say about the importance of Indigenous journalists/journalism in regards to healing?

“Walker sees exposing the truth of what happened as a vital first step before healing can occur.” (VIU, 2021).

What does Connie Walker say about the importance of making spaces for Indigenous experiences in the media?

“It’s absolutely critical that the space is created so these stories can get out as far as possible, because if people are not being told, then no healing is ever going to happen,” she [Connie Walker] says. (VIU, 2021)
