Module 9: Sharing our stories

Taking components of Indigenous traditions and adapting them to meet the current needs and desires of non-Indigenous people is known as cultural appropriation. This practice of appropriating and distorting the representation of Indigenous Peoples and cultures has been going on for more than a century.

Appropriation takes many forms, and often, it occurs under the guise of "honouring" Indigenous Peoples. In fact, it denigrates them. Examples include lifting iconography and performative activities from Indigenous cultures and using these without consultation or permission. More recently, it has sometimes taken the form of assuming an Indigenous identity for the purpose of gaining financial advantage.

Numerous iconography and performative activities are used to denigrate Indigenous people. In this module, you will learn about how colonial narratives work to maintain control over Indigenous representation, why this is done, and who benefits from this control. In light of this, you will also consider how and why it is important for Indigenous people to tell their own stories and share their experiences.
