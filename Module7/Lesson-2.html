Journal Questions

What is ‘I’m Not the Indian You Had in Mind’ trying to expose about Indigenous stereotypes? How does Indigenous media differ from mainstream Canadian media?

In ‘I’m Not the Indian You Had in Mind’, they say, “sometimes late at night when all the world is warm and dead, [I] wonder how might’ve been had you followed and we led”. Imagine how the world might be different if that were the case. What problems do we face today that might not exist?

Why is the visibility of Indigenous narratives so important in making changes to the current issues that Indigenous Peoples face?

Why is it important that Indigenous People be the ones to tell Indigenous stories?

Before watching ‘The “Indian Problem”’ had you heard of ‘The Indian Removal Act’ (1830)? Connect this to how you were (mis)taught North American history through media or schooling.

Deeper Thinking: How was ‘The Indian Removal Act’ (1830) a form of genocide and cultural genocide? How does the legacy of ‘The Indian Removal Act’ live on today?

Document your responses to the questions above. Feel free to use any format to complete this exercise
