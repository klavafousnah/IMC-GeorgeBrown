Module 7: Indigenous Activism

Throughout Turtle Island, Indigenous Peoples have been on a path of reclaiming their histories and raising their concerns for the better part of the last century. Indigenous people have been aware of the treatment and attitudes directed toward them by settlers. They are aware of the policies and laws that have been put in place to weaken their Indigenous rights. Indigenous peoples have resisted colonization and assimilation in both individual and collective acts.

Indigenous People's habitation of the land is at the heart of the conflict between settlers and Indigenous People. Settlers saw untapped resource potential and desired to develop it all. The settlers, on the other hand, failed or did not care to see the vast and complex coordination in place, where there were sovereign Nations, an economic system, and movement understood between the Nations. This module investigates how Indigenous peoples have reclaimed their narratives and taken control of their stories, histories, and ongoing relationship with Canada.

Why are stereotypes harmful?

Stereotypes paint a monolithic picture of an entire group of people. Beyond that, they often reinforce negative and false narratives about groups of people. They can also negatively impact peoples understanding of their own identity.
