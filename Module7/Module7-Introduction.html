Learning Objectives

By the end of the module you will be able to:

  • Explain how Indigenous art and music represent Indigenous activism.
  • Identify how the mainstream media limits Indigenous voices.
  • Analyze contemporary assertions of Indigenous sovereignty, self-determination, and sustainability by Indigenous artists.

Module 7: Indigenous Activism

Governments and corporations have continued to take resources from the traditional territories of Indigenous Peoples without consultation, or consent, and in most instances have not shared royalties with Indigenous communities. Land degradation and further oppression of Indigenous Peoples remain important issues today.

To right these wrongs, Indigenous Peoples have long advocated for Indigenous rights and engaged in activism that has only grown stronger with each decade. There have always been Indigenous-led movements to bring awareness and education to the Canadian population. In this module, we will examine how some Indigenous artists, in particular, musicians, have used their platforms and voices to bring awareness to Indigenous issues and to address the discrimination of Indigenous Peoples across Turtle Island.

Why are stereotypes harmful?

Stereotypes paint a monolithic picture of an entire group of people. Beyond that, they often reinforce negative and false narratives about groups of people. They can also negatively impact peoples understanding of their own identity.
