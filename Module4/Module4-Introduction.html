Module 4: Indigenous Perspectives on Treaties

Objectives

  • Analyze the concept of privilege.
  • Apply concepts of responsibility to community development.
  • Explain how the media portrays the relationship between land and identity within Indigenous societies.

Introduction to Module 4

The Nation-to-Nation Relationships were established between the Indigenous Nation and the Canadian Government. There were two perspectives on Indigenous Peoples' and settlers' relationships. The Two Row Wampum depicts how the two Nations, with two beaded purple rows running lengthwise, were to travel in their own canoes and not steer into the path of the other. The Treaties were interpreted in this way by Indigenous Nations, but this was not the intention of the Treaties.

[Insert the Two Row Wampum photo]

This module will look at current and historical examples of racism as well as popular representations of Indigenous Peoples. Students will describe Canadian perceptions of Treaties and how the land and Treaties shape Indigenous perspectives. In the early 1990s, the Oka Crisis altered the political landscape and solidified how the Canadian government interacts with Indigenous People. More blockades occurred, and reports have been released detailing how the Canadian government treated Indigenous people in Canada.
