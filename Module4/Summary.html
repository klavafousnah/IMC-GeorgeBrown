Module 4: Indigenous Perspectives on Treaties

Summary

Objectives

  • Explain how the media portrays the relationship between land and identity within Indigenous societies.
  • Analyze racism inherent in the media in relation to Indigenous peoples.
  • Develop a complex understanding of treaties from multiple historical and personal narratives.

Summary

This module introduced you to Treaties and the way they historically and currently impact Indigenous peoples. Treaty narratives told by settlers have, in part, determined how Indigenous peoples are (mis)represented throughout the media. You should be able to make connections between power, privilege, oppression, and the exploitive actions of British settlers.

Test your Knowledge

What is a ‘treaty’?

An agreement between two sovereign nations

How was the Oka crisis an example of colonization?

During the Oka Crisis the Canadian government aided the attempt of developers to occupy and build upon Mohawk land.
