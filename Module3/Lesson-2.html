Introduction to Privilege Is Power: How You Can Use It to Do Some Good! and What Non-Indigenous Canadians Need to Know

Today’s dominant culture is based on colonialism. Turtle Island was colonized through European expansion into territories already inhabited by Indigenous Peoples. This expansion was undertaken for the purposes of taking land and resources, and based on the notion of terra nullius (Latin for “land of no one”). The notion that a human being could be “no one” is the basis for our present-day social structures of privilege and oppression.

The social identities of people marginalized in our society include all racialized people (Black, Indigenous, and People of Colour), people with disabilities, people who are LGBTQ2S+, people who experience poverty, and people with mental-health issues. The list is long. Understanding privilege means being able to identify which of our social identities are not marginalized, consider how we have benefitted from this, and then work toward making change in our society.
