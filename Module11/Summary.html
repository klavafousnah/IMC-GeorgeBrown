Module 11: Weaving Through ReconciliationUnderstand different perspectives of reconciliation

Summary

Objectives

  • Understand different perspectives of reconciliation
  • Understand how Indigenous medias amplify Indigenous voices and narratives
  • Identify how Indigenous medias differ from mainstream media

Summary

This module explored reconciliation and how Indigenous medias empower and amplify Indigenous Peoples. You should now understand what reconciliation means to the Canadian government, politicians, and Indigenous Peoples; you should be able to compare and contrast these understandings. Additionally, you should now understand the importance of Indigenous medias and their role in Indigenous healing, sovereignty, and rights.

Test your Knowledge

What is the dictionary definition of ‘reconciliation’?

“[singular, uncountable] Reconciliation (between A and B) reconciliation (with somebody) an end to a disagreement and the start of a good relationship again.”

What is the dictionary definition of restitution?

Restitution (of something) (to somebody/something) (formal) is the act of giving back something that was lost or stolen to its owner.
