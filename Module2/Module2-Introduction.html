Module 2: Creation Story

Objectives

  • Analyze the role of mainstream media in perpetuating the colonial narrative that adversely impacts Indigenous communities.
  • Analyze racism inherent in the media in relation to Indigenous peoples.

Introducution to Module 2

The purpose of this module is to gain an understanding of the significance of Indigenous Knowledges, the construction of settler narratives, and the ongoing colonization of Indigenous Nations. Students will begin developing their ability to conduct a critical analysis of colonial narratives’ stories and perceptions that have a detrimental effect on Indigenous communities.

Indigenous Knowledge is a broad term that refers to knowledge generated within a community to ensure survival and decision-making for the next Seven Generations. To ensure the long-term well-being of the Indigenous Nation and the ability to make sound decisions, Indigenous Peoples comprehend the world in various ways, including through observation, listening, and sharing stories. This module will provide an overview of Indigenous Knowledges and their relationship to Indigenous storytelling.

Indigenous Knowledges are multifaceted: It shapes the Indigenous worldview, which includes the body of Knowledge of origin stories, ways of life, and ways of being. There are a variety of terms used by Indigenous Peoples to describe their unique methods of knowing, being, and doing. Each nation will have its own set of Original Instructions that will govern how they interact with the land. It is critical to recognize that these can be derived from history, legends, stories, and ceremonial practices.
