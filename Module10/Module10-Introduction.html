MODULE 10: RISE OF INDIGENOUS REPORTING

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module you will be able to:

  • Analyze the impact of media bias and manipulation of information on Indigenous people and their stories.
  • Identify the difference when Indigenous stories are reported by Indigenous reporters.
  • Explain why Indigenous journalists’ coverage of missing and murdered Indigenous women and girls is important.
  • Explain the impact of colonial narratives that perpetuate misinformation, racism, and stereotypes on both Indigenous communities and the broader society.

    • End of the week check: I am able to identify why Indigenous reporting is important.

Module 10: Rise of Indigenous Reporting

Indigenous people continue to be portrayed in a negative light in the media, and critical Indigenous moments have been poorly covered. Journalists need to have more knowledge of Indigenous issues to competently report on them in a way that does not further colonial narratives. One of the most infamous, exploited Missing and Murdered Indigenous women is Pocahontas, who has been hypersexualized by the Disney move. Settlers changed the narrative of a child who was kidnapped and died at a young age to one of being in love with her captor.

Indigenous journalism is becoming increasingly important and popular as a means of presenting accurate information and insights on issues affecting Indigenous Peoples. Furthermore, many Indigenous advocates, artists, journalists, and writers have adopted social media to express themselves and shed light on Indigenous experiences and perspectives. This has significantly influenced modern discourse about key issues of importance to Indigenous Peoples. It has contributed to not only information sharing but education of the wider public.

In this module, you will experience Indigenous people telling stories in an authentic and meaningful way. Indigenous Peoples have an established record of representing our worldviews. Now we see Indigenous content creators emerging in all facets of media production including theatre, film, literature, documentaries, and social media.

Why are Indigenous new sources, like APTN, significant?

Indigenous Peoples having control over telling their own narratives takes power back from settlers who have (mis)told Indigenous stories for years. It also allows for the important integration of Indigenous cultures and languages into media.

Why are disappearances and murders of Indigenous people often mishandled by police?

Multiple correct answers. For example, policing is an institution that is built upon white supremacy and racism. Therefore, policing usually favours white people and criminalizes Indigenous Peoples and cases of Indigenous disappearances and murders are ignored or under-investigated.
